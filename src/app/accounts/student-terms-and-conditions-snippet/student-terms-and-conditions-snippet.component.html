INFORMED CONSENT: STUDENT PARTICIPATION

Introduction

You are invited to participate in a study that concerns how people interact educational software and/or output of educational software. As a participant in this study, you will be asked to complete a questionnaire and interact with educational software as needed.
This statement contains information about the present study that is intended to help you decide whether you wish to participate in it. You may refuse to sign this form and not participate in this study. You should be aware that even after you signed, you are free to withdraw at any time. If you withdraw from this study, it will not affect your relationship with this unit, the people involved in the study, the services it may provide to you, or the University of British Columbia Okanagan. If you have any questions about this study before, during, or after your participation, please feel free to direct them to:
Dr. Bowen Hui
Tel: 250-807-9353, Email: bowen.hui@ubc.ca

Study Objectives

The objective of this work is to develop educational software that helps students and course administrators to automatically generate and interpret feedback from assignment submissions in a course.

Procedure: Software Feedback Usefulness (Total Expected Time: 0.5 hours)

  1. As part of the course, you will work on assignments and submit them as usual. Nothing will change from standard procedures.
  2. You can use the software as much or as little as you need to in order to complete the course work required by your instructor. There are many educational activities available in the software that you can use throughout the semester.
  3. At the end of the course, you will be asked to complete a short questionnaire that helps us identify what was useful for you and what changes you would like to see in the future.

Risks

There are no known risks to participants that as there is nothing more than what you would normally be doing for studying purposes regardless of their participation in the study.

Benefits

The participants may gain a better understanding of the concepts that you practice on through the software, may develop an appreciation for learning analytics software, and may develop interest in the research and development process.

Privacy and Confidentiality

Questionnaires and computer logs are anonymous. Names of participants will be stored separately from information collected by a neutral third party from UBC Okanagan IT department, Olivier Dube. Therefore, the instructor of the course will not get access to participants’ information until after the course appeal date. During this time, the instructor will only have access to anonymized version of your data. Information collected include all your electronically submitted programming work and associated marks (such as labs and assignments). Your name will not be associated in any way with the information collected about you or your usage of the computer during the study. Furthermore, because the interest of this study is to identify average responses and behavior of the entire group of participants, you will not be identified in any way in any written reports of this research.
In the case that your instructor provides a bonus mark incentive to participating in this study, the list of participants will be provided to the instructor to update your course grade. To get the bonus mark, you may opt to not participate and complete an alternate assignment instead. In the case that your instructor does not provide a bonus mark incentive for participation, no information is given to the instructor whether you participated or not.
Participants have a right to access their own results so long as their names and numeric tags are still available. The data obtained from both components of the study will be retained for at least 5 years after publication in secured, electronic form, which only researchers associated with this research will have access to. No audio or video tapes will be used. General results of the research study may be available through publications. A summary of the results will be made available to participants upon request.

Access to Course Work and Associated Grades

By checking the boxes below, you are providing consent for the researchers in this study to analyze your course work and grades. The grades will only be used for research analysis purposes without including any of the participants’ personal information. You can participate in this study by giving us access to your submitted course work, the associated grades, or both. In the case you decided to not participate in this study, you will be provided with an alternative assignment for getting the bonus mark.
